Punctuation Essentials

Commas

Use after introductory phrases, between items in a list, and to join two clauses with a conjunction.

After the experiment, the data were compiled, checked, and shared.

Semicolons

Link two related independent clauses or separate complex list items.

The policy passed; public support was higher than expected.

Colons

Introduce a list, explanation, or quotation that follows a complete sentence.

Three forces drive the outcome: funding, timing, and coordination.

Em dashes

Insert emphasis or an abrupt break in thought—use sparingly.

The result was clear—intervention cut delays in half.
